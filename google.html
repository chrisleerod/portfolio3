Google Exercise

Helping new students connect with experienced students.

Roles

Product design

Platform

Mobile

Time period

Feb 12-19, 2020

View Process Deck

New students struggle to adjust to campus life and get their questions answered.

Getting questions answered related to classes, majors, guidance, advice, and other like-minded topics should not be a difficult process. This can be solved by promoting community-oriented interactions between students.

This design exercise is my take on the problem mentioned above. It was completed over a seven-day design sprint in response to Google’s UX internship design challenge.

Introduction

I applied to Google’s 2020 UX summer internship and was instructed to choose between two exercises to design a solution for. I was given a seven-day deadline to research, wireframe, and design my product.

The challenge

“Your school wants to strengthen the community by encouraging experienced students to connect with new students and help them adjust to campus life. Design an experience that allows mentors and mentees to discover each other. Consider the needs of both mentors and mentees, including how someone may become a mentor and how to connect mentors to mentees.”

Defining the sprint

After understanding the prompt and deadline, I decided to use Google Venture’s design sprint methodology to stay on top of the short deadline and have pre-determined goals. I used it as a general guideline and tweaked it according to my needs and limitations.

01 Understand

This phase was very important. It would lay out the ground work for the entire project. In order to understand the problem thoroughly, I asked some critical questions.

How might we allow students to connect and discover other students and mentors, become mentors, and satisfy the needs of both mentors and mentees with MentorU?

How do current first-year students seek guidance? How do they connect with other students? How do they discover new opportunities?

After forming a couple preliminary questions, I interviewed students and conducted a survey to collect both quantitative and qualitative data. This allowed me to generate user insights, personas, and better empathize with fellow students.
